Victor Stommendal

So, you want to know more about me – then you’re in the right place! I’m Victor, an engineering student at Chalmers University of Technology, currently studying Global Systems. I was born and raised in Trollhättan, Sweden – a city with a strong connection to engineering and industry. Growing up in Trollhättan, I was surrounded by my family, including my parents and my older brother. They have played an important role in shaping who I am today, and I could probably write an entire chapter about the experiences, memories, and lessons they have given me. However, this page is not meant to be a full autobiography (maybe that will be a future project), so I will leave it at saying that I am very grateful for the support and inspiration they have given me throughout my life. My older brother has always been one of the people I look up to the most. He has a deep interest in physics and astronomy, and ever since we were young, he has dreamed of becoming an astronaut—a goal he continues to pursue after completing the M.Sc. programme in Engineering Physics at Chalmers University of Technology in 2026. Growing up, we shared a strong curiosity about the world around us. Many evenings were spent gazing at the stars, discussing space, and philosophizing about life, the universe, and how everything works. Those conversations sparked countless questions and helped nurture the curiosity and desire to understand complex systems that still motivate me today.

I have always been curious about how things work and, perhaps even more importantly, how different parts influence each other. This curiosity is what led me towards engineering and eventually to Global Systems – a programme focused on understanding complex interactions between technology, society, and natural systems. During my studies, I have developed a strong interest in data analysis, programming, modelling, and using engineering methods to solve complex problems. Through experiences at companies such as Volvo Group and GKN Aerospace, I have gained insight into how engineering is applied in real industrial environments, from system architecture to technology analysis and data visualization.

Outside engineering, I enjoy exploring the world around me – both literally and scientifically. I have travelled to over 20 countries, experiencing different cultures, environments, and ways of thinking. Travelling has taught me to be adaptable, curious, and open to new perspectives, which I believe are valuable qualities both personally and professionally. Beside travelling, I enjoy staying active and spending time outdoors. Training has always been an important part of my life, whether it is strength training at the gym, running, skiing, or playing squash. I also have a strong connection to nature, and during the warmer months you will often find me fishing, exploring the outdoors, spending time with friends and family, or relaxing at my family’s summer house outside Mellerud in Dalsland. For me, these activities are not only about performance or achievement, but also about finding balance, challenging myself, and appreciating the simple moments in everyday life.

As a person, I would describe myself as driven, calm, humble, easygoing, and caring. Among my friends, I have the nickname “Stommen”, which loosely translates to “The Core” or “The Foundation”. While it does not directly describe my personality, I know that many people around me see me as someone steady, reliable, and someone they can count on. I enjoy working together with others and believe that the best solutions often come from combining different perspectives. At the same time, I have a competitive side that has followed me from sports into engineering projects and everyday challenges.

Looking ahead, I hope to work at the intersection between technology, data, and sustainable development. I am especially interested in how engineering and data-driven solutions can help solve some of the complex challenges facing society today.
